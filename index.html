QRAM

Qram stands for "Queued Rebase and Merge" and rhymes nicely with "cram", nudging you to work more on meaningful things and leave cramming commits into neat chains to robots.

Much like Gitlab's Merge trains and Github's recently introduced Merge queues, Qram strives to provide a solution for testing multiple PRs in conjunction to detect conflicts, but targets a specific opinionated workflow:

  • commit history in PRs matter, so they get merged and not squashed;
  • linear history in main branch matters too, so PRs have to be rebased on top of each other.
